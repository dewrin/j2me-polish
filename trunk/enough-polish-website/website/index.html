polish

Use polish for your J2ME-GUI development.

Problems of the Java 2 Microedition

The Java 2 Microedition is the standard for the development of mobile applications, but it brings several problems with it:

  • fragmentation of the J2ME market.
    There a zillions of J2ME-enabled devices out there and everyone of it has special features, limitations and bugs!
  • GUI design is... tough!
    The design of the GUI of J2ME-applications is complex, to say the least. So the average designer has great difficulties designing a J2ME-application. The usual solution is, that the programmer of the business logic also needs to design the application. To be honest, this is not the best solution!
  • No seperation of design and development.
    Designers cannot apply their knowledge without the help of the developers in J2ME applications. Regularily the programmer will develop the application and when the application has been finished, a designer will give some hints how to improve the design and usability of the application. This causes often quite a headache. Why can't developers and designers work independently at the programs?

polish is the solution!

polish allows you to keep in pace with the zillions of J2ME-devices. The design of J2ME-applications is done with CSS-files, so it's really easy and designers can work on the design independently of the developers!

Mailing lists

polish has several mailing lists for discussions etc:

  • users: please join the polish-users@lists.berlios.de-list.
    In this list users can discuss their problems. The developers of the polish framework will monitor the discussions and help out!
  • announcements: please join the polish-announce@lists.berlios.de-list for retrieving news regarding the polish framework.
    This list anounces new versions and has very low traffic.
  • developers: please join the polish-devel@lists.berlios.de-list.
    This is for the developers of the polish framework.
